PROBLEM STATEMENT

A majority of the young adult generation are affected by loneliness, boredom and unproductive lifestyle due to the lockdown of pandemic. This situation can be much worse for the single person living alone at one place for long.

PROJECT GOALS

The high level goals were to -

  • Create personal assistant experience to provide empathetic interaction
  • Create interactive user interface to improve user engagement and user’s productivity
  • Create a new visual appearance of Amazon Alexa by integrate Alexa voice assistant and mobile app to provide delightful experience to user

BACKGROUND

Amazon's Alexa is one of the popular of digital AI assistant. Alexa has the potential to serve the user more efficiently, as it is already well appreciated by user for its amazing household task. Due to the lack of interactive communication with Alexa rather than the voice command, people are mostly using it for controlling smart home devices. Adding the non-verbal communication with Alexa along with verbal communication and responsibilities like personal assistant can increase the user engagement. The concept comes from my post graduate research on analyzing user engagement with virtual AI assistant. Having both verbal and non-verbal communication interactive visual appearance with gestures and emotion along with the voice) can improve the engagement during the user interaction

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

[Needs]

  1. What are essentials for the engaging and interactive interaction with Alexa?
  2. What are the user's current pain points to interact with Alexa app?

[Behaviours]

  1. How do people deal with the boredom and loneliness in lockdown?
  2. How do people use Alexa in lockdown time and before?
  3. Do user's really need empathetic interaction with Alexa?

RESEARCH GOALS

  • I want to understand how to make the user interaction more engaging and empathetic that can aid in user's boredom and loneliness and what are their expectations from Alexa mobile app to serve as personal assistant.
  • I want to find out the appropriate scope to add non verbal communication with Alexa when it response with voice.

SECONDARY RESEARCH

VOICE ASSISTANT USAGE

I began my research by looking at the research paper, article and statistics. I wanted to understand their experience with AI assistant, emotion, and effect in their daily life.

  • 2 in 5 adults use voice search once daily. (Location World)
  • 25% of individuals ages 16–24 use voice search on mobile. (Global Web Index)
  • 41% of people who own a voice-activated speaker say it feels like talking to a friend or another person. (Google)

In a nutshell young adults are the main consumers of voice assistant and they have very intimate experiences with smart devices.

ALEXA APP ISSUES

I came across a lot of user reviews and came to the conclusion that users had very similar frustrations: 

  • Alexa’s task performance and conversation flow were 20% less than google.
  • Alexa doesn’t work outside the home.
  • Alexa’s absence on mobile devices.

    • "It’s omnipresent in households, but I don’t know anyone who uses it instead of Siri or Google Assistant on their phone"
    "I can’t summon the digital assistant with the regular “Alexa” on my iPhone alexa app, so I mostly use the app to tweak Alexa settings."

In general, these puts a limit on its ability to function as a daily helper. People line on their phone and any assistance need to be there too.

LONELINESS BEFORE AND DURING COVID

Isolation, Loneliness, and COVID-19: Pandemic leads to sharp increase in mental health challenges, social woes. Young Canadian people are more likely to feel lonely than their older peers. One-in-three between the ages of 18 and 34 (34%) are in this most lonely. (Source)

According to the latest COVID Response Tracking Study conducted by NORC at the University of Chicago,
"A majority of Americans ages 18 through 34, (56%) are likely to report feeling isolated during pandemic."

Besides Voice Assistants already saw an uptick in daily use during the pandemic. (Voicebot.ai)

PRIMARY RESEARCH

INTERVIEW STUDY

I have recruited participants through snowball sampling. And, conducted 20-minute remote semi-structured interview session with 4 participants to gather in-depth qualitative data. From the interview the overall observations are-

  • People are unhappy with the interaction experience and unintuitive user interface of the mobile app.
  • People think Alexa is less of an assistant more of a device controller.

From the user review from secondary research and user interview , it is clear that users had very similar frustrations:

  1. Unintuitive user interface and interaction experience.
  2. Alexa doesn’t work outside the home.
  3. Can’t summon the digital assistant from iphone.
SURVEY STUDY

I have collected data from 15 people through E-survey. The participants were from 20-35 years old, use Amazon Alexa (may use google home also) and single person at home.

Key findings:

  • 45% participants reported Alexa's conversation flow is not very user friendly and interactive.
  • 57% participants reported they would like to see more interactive facility in the mobile app other than voice command also.
