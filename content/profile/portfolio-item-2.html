PROBLEM STATEMENT

Although customers can find them by word of mouth but realized they are loosing their leads and unable to inform about their specialized service to a broader audience because of the lack of online presence. Customers remain unaware of the daily/ weekly/ special offers, latest items and available items in stock until they visit in person.

BACKGROUND

Cupcake crave, a small bakery makes homemade, organic cupcakes located in Vancouver, BC. Their focus is to offer outstanding design with freshly baked quality ingredients cupcake. Currently they only have a small shop. They are facing difficulties to grow and maintain their buisness motive, their current buisness strategy making it hard to keep up with the customer statisfaction as well. They are looking for an alternative media where they can promote their services and customers can know about the business and services, watch menu online and place order.

DISCOVER

UNDERSTANDING THE MARKET

I began my research by looking at independent bakery market trends that serves cupcakes, which helped me understand the local market and begin thinking about Cupcake Crave's target market. I researched local market competition, and looked at the websites and customer reviews. I highlighted a few trends:

  1. Presenting products attractively, in a clean, well-lit case, will help foods sell themselves.
  2. Community building is a top priority.
  3. Gluten free bakery is one of the top seller and represents a key sector of bakery market needs.
  4. Customized items need to offer options that are gluten-free, vegan, or paleo to reach larger audience.

Competitive Analysis

I looked at 3 direct competitors that are bakery shop in close proximity. They have a focus on cupcake items and customized services or at least serve cupcake as a major product and have opportunity to increase their cupcake section. Though all of them have their website but either online order is not available for cupcake or the online/pre order costs much than regular bought cupcake.

USER INTERVIEW ANALYSIS

Next, I spoke to three individuals in the target demographic (20-35 years old, teenage and young professional, living in or near a city) to gain a better understanding of users’ need, goal and frustrations. Some highlights of the findings are-

FRUSTRATIONS
  • Difficult to know what special item or offer the store has for today and what they are going to have on upcoming day without visiting in person.
  • Store already runs out of the favourite flavour of the customer when customer reach there.
  • Doesn’t have time always to visit the store to buy cupcake for an occasion.
  • A long wait to be served the ordered items if the order amount is large.
  • Doesn’t have time to wait in line in hurry and find the anticipted item is not available after waiting in line.
EXPECTATIONS
  • Participants are willing to pay more for baked goods if it is high-quality.
  • Store already runs out of the favourite flavour of the customer when customer reach there.
  • Participants would like to have the vegan, gluten free and other diet restricted items available in any time constraint.
  • Participants want the option to customize items without compromising the quality and taste.
  • Participants would like to purchase baked goods on a one-off basis online or in person and delivered to home on expected date.

DESIGN

I have followed a set of Gestalt Principles of Design to guide visual design decision

Similarity

Grouped the cupcake item cards in the menue by color, shape, and size.

Proximity

Grouped similiar category cupake menu cards together without using hard borders but including space in between each category to reflect their category structure.

Closure

Showed partial image of the daily flavour items fading off the screen in order to depict that there is more to be found if they swipe right.

Continuation

Navigation bar contains tabs selections with a partial option provides an affordance that there are additional options or interactions.

USABILITY TESTING

I tested the intercative prototype with three users. I asked them to complete a couple of tasks in order to test navigating the website.

You have heard about good things of cupcake cake’s cupcake. So, you would like to try some cupcakes from there store, you go to the website to find out their menu, hours of operation and the ordering procedure. You have liked a cupcake and place the order from website for home delivery.

Through usability testing the prototype, a few patterns quickly emerged.

Problems and Solutions:
Highlight Store Hours
I had included the store hours in the footer of the site, believing that this was a common design pattern to which users would navigate naturally. But it was important to highlight this information at the very begining because partiicpants were looking for this information before scrolling down to the footer.
Static Cupcake Catagory
Participants were clicking to the cupcake catagory options on daily flavours by thinking those are buttons to view that particular catagory rather scrolling down. But, when they clicked the icons nothing happened, then they scrolled down the page to view menu of each catagory. So, I have changed the visual design of the catagory option.
Reposition Gift Coupon
While going through the payment process, participants were clicking on the gift coupon apply button before entering the customer/guest information. But, gift coupon should come while entering the payment method informtaion. So, I have disabled the option until reaching to the payment method information stage.
