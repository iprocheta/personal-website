Evaluate Gender Stereotypes in Embodied AI Agents

RESEARCH SUMMARY

This research is intended as an effort towards a progressive and inclusive approach for gender representations in virtual agents. 
- Created standardized and representative Male, Female and Androgynous embodied agents characters through iterative design process by manipulating visual cues. 
- Evaluated these agents with the stereotypical assumptions of gendered traits and roles in AI virtual agents

RESEARCH QUESTIONS

When only varying with binary gender related appearance of embodied virtual agents (ECAs), how does the experience of the user change?
Moving past binary distinctions of male and female, how does the more fluid notion of gender (or even gender neutral) affect user's experience with ECAs?
How does the gender-related appearance of male, female and androgynous agents impact the user perception in terms of gender stereotypes traits?

RESEARCH OBJECTIVES

Examine the effect of gender during a user's conversation with the embodied conversational agent (ECA).
Examine the user experience on perceiving realistic 3D ECA characters, where all other factors are kept as uniform as possible, to measure for differences in ECA visual gender cues only.
Examine the effect of the agents' gender-related appearance: male, female and androgynous, on user perception in terms of gender stereotype traits and roles.

Issues With Past Studies

  1. Many studies use vastly different kinds of embodied character agents and just simply call them avatars from stick figures to illustrations to 2D to fully 3D.
  2. Additional confounding factors of agent characters.
  3. Confounding factors (race, tallness, weight, hair colour) to assure that users are seeing the same general agent other than gender difference.
  4. No studies on gender stereotypes, examining the core reason.
  5. Only binary notion of gender used to validate the gender stereotype assumption.

THEORY

Gender stereotypes have been found in virtual agent research  (Gulz et al., 2008,… …) ;     However,  Gender Stereotypes are Changing for Humans. New study (Eagly et al.,2020). 

Methodology

I followed a two-stage methodology to resolve the issue of study 1 and many other past studies.

HYPOTHESIS

  1. Communion traits would be rated significantly higher for the agents that are perceived as female, followed by androgynous and male agents.
  2. Agency traits would be rated significantly lower for the agents that are perceived as female, followed by androgynous and male agents.
  3. Competence traits would not be significantly different between agents that are perceived as more female, androgynous and male.

Study 1: Character Design Process

First Iteration of Character Design

At first , I created 6 characters to represent 3 gender categories. Unlike the previous studies, I tried to design these characters with a minimal amount of changes in the visual attributes,. That’s why I removed the effect of clothing, skin colour, hair colour, eye colour, height, body type, and different facial expressions for different agents.

Second Iteration of Character Design

Removed overlooked features Adam's apple, wide neck and fuller eyelashes.

Study 2: Evaluate based on stereotypical traits and role

Study Design

Analysis

  Quantitative method

Within subject design with counterbalanced

  Independent and Dependent variables

IV: Gendered representation of ECAs (3 levels: Male, Female and Androgynous) Gender of participants (2 levels: Male and Female).
DV: Agency, communion and competence and roles.

  Participants

72 participants in total from Amazon Mturk, Male=41, Female=31, Age= 25-34 (majority).

Stereotype Traits

Communion : affectionate, compassionate, sensitive, and emotional
Agency : ambitious, aggressive, courageous, and decisive
Competence : creative, intelligent, innovative and organized

Stereotype Roles

Female-dominated roles : Health coach and Personal assistant
Male-dominated roles : Exercise coach and Financial advisor
Gender balanced roles :  News reporter and Storyteller

Statistical analysis

Two way 2 (participant’s gender: male and female) *3 ( agent’s gender: male, female and androgynous) mixed ANOVA with repeated measures

RESULTS

CONCLUSION

Discussion

  • Representative of male, female and androgynous agents with minimal differences.
  • Androgynous agents could be used to achieve a middle ground of the male and female gender.
  • Gender-balanced view of agency, communion, competence traits.
  • Roles could be used interchangeably, even with androgynous agents

Contribution

  • Rigorous design process to create visual design balance
  • Minimize confounding features
  • Introducing androgyny
  • Changing gender stereotypes notion reflects on virtual agent
